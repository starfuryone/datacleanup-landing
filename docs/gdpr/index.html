GDPR Compliance

Full compliance with EU General Data Protection Regulation and your privacy rights

Your Privacy Rights Under GDPR

As a data processor and controller, DataCleanup Pro is fully committed to GDPR compliance. We respect your privacy rights and have implemented comprehensive measures to ensure the protection of your personal data in accordance with EU law.

Right to Information

You have the right to know how we process your personal data.

  • Clear privacy notices and policies
  • Transparent data collection practices
  • Information about data retention periods
  • Details about third-party data sharing

Right of Access

You can request access to your personal data we hold.

  • Free copy of your personal data
  • Information about processing purposes
  • Details about data recipients
  • Source of the data (if not collected from you)

Right to Rectification

You can correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

  • Update incorrect personal information
  • Complete incomplete data records
  • Notification to third parties where applicable
  • Response within one month

Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten").

  • Delete data when no longer necessary
  • Withdraw consent for processing
  • Object to unlawful processing
  • Comply with legal obligations

Right to Restrict Processing

Limit how we process your personal data in certain situations.

  • Contest accuracy of personal data
  • Processing is unlawful
  • Data needed for legal claims
  • Objection to processing pending verification

Right to Data Portability

Receive your personal data in a machine-readable format.

  • Structured, commonly used format
  • Transfer to another controller
  • Automated processing based on consent
  • No adverse effect on others' rights

Right to Object

Object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.

  • Stop direct marketing communications
  • Object to automated decision-making
  • Challenge legitimate interest processing
  • Opt-out of profiling activities

Rights Related to Automated Decision Making

Protection from automated decisions with significant effects.

  • Human intervention in decision processes
  • Express your point of view
  • Contest automated decisions
  • Request manual review

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on the following legal grounds under GDPR:

Article 6(1)(b) - Contract

Processing necessary for the performance of our Terms of Service contract, including providing data cleaning services, managing your account, and processing payments.

Article 6(1)(a) - Consent

With your explicit consent for marketing communications, newsletter subscriptions, and optional data processing features like AI categorization.

Article 6(1)(f) - Legitimate Interest

For our legitimate interests in improving our services, preventing fraud, ensuring security, and conducting business analytics, balanced against your privacy rights.

Article 6(1)(c) - Legal Obligation

To comply with legal requirements such as tax regulations, anti-money laundering laws, and court orders.

Data Protection Measures

  • End-to-end encryption (TLS/SSL and AES-256)
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Regular security audits and assessments
  • Staff training on data protection
  • Data breach notification procedures
  • Privacy by design and by default

International Data Transfers

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Adequacy decisions where applicable
  • Additional safeguards for non-EEA transfers
  • Regular assessment of transfer mechanisms
  • Documentation of all data transfers
  • Impact assessments for high-risk transfers

Data Breach Procedures

In accordance with GDPR Article 33 and 34, we have established comprehensive data breach procedures:

  • Immediate incident response and containment within 1 hour
  • Risk assessment and impact evaluation within 24 hours
  • Notification to supervisory authority within 72 hours
  • Individual notification without undue delay if high risk
  • Detailed breach documentation and lessons learned
  • Regular testing of incident response procedures
